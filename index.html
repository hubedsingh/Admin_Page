International Students Admission Schemes

International students can apply under the following two schemes:

  1. DASA (Direct Admission of Students Abroad):

    This initiative by the Government of India (GOI) is for Foreign Nationals, NRIs (Non-resident Indians), PIOs (Persons of Indian Origin), & Overseas Citizens of India (OCI). NIT Hamirpur will be one of the participating institutions under the DASA category for Admissions 2024-25 to the B.Tech. and M.Tech. programs. Candidates eligible & interested to participate under the DASA scheme (Wait for updates for 2024).

  2. ICCR (Indian Council for Cultural Relations Scholarship):

    International students can apply through ICCR for U.G, P.G., and Ph.D. programs. For more details, visit ICCR Scholarships.

Life in Bharat

Discover why India is one of the best destinations in the world.

Comprises 28 states and 8 union territories. India is the third-largest Asian nation and the largest democratic nation in the world. India has about 1,632 different languages and dialects, and the Constitution lists 22 official languages. India has been a rich hub of multiculturalism and cultural diversity. It is home to policies of legal pluralism in religious family law (Hindu, Muslim, Christian, and Parsi to name a few), territorial autonomy for several linguistic and indigenous groups, safeguarding the rights of sexual and gender minorities, as well as quotas in legislatures, government jobs, and educational institutions for caste and tribal minorities. The Indian Constitution has been hailed as a prescient model of multicultural accommodation for its recognition of a range of group-differentiated rights within a broadly liberal democratic framework.

India has an eventful history and is widely known and respected worldwide. It is also revered in the East as the land of wisdom and came to be known as the land of wealth in the West. India’s grandeur was widely acknowledged by the name of ‘Son Chidiya’ that was given to it because of its existing wealth and diversity of culture. Another aspect of Indian culture is the architecture and the art of the country. India has always had a variety of architecture like Hindu architecture, Indo-Islamic architecture, Rajput architecture, etc. Monuments such as the Taj Mahal, Chittor Fort, Lotus Temple, etc. depict the vastness of the culture and centuries of history.

Life in Himachal Pradesh

Located in the northern part of India, Himachal Pradesh is known for its stunning snow-capped mountains and lush green valleys. The state is a treasure trove of cultural heritage, reflected in its local folklore, traditional dance forms like Nati, and religious diversity with Hinduism and Buddhism being predominant. Himachal Pradesh has produced numerous freedom fighters, such as Major Somnath Sharma, who was posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra.

The state's economy is primarily driven by agriculture and horticulture, particularly known for its apples, peaches, and apricots. Popular towns such as Shimla, Manali, and Dharamshala attract both national and international tourists. Himachal Pradesh is also home to significant pilgrimage sites, including Jakhoo Temple in Shimla and the Tibetan monasteries in McLeod Ganj, making it a spiritual haven for travelers.

About Hamirpur

  • Hamirpur is a district located in the western part of Himachal Pradesh and is known for its scenic landscapes and rich cultural history.
  • The town of Hamirpur is a growing educational hub, hosting the prestigious National Institute of Technology (NIT) and several other renowned institutes.
  • The region boasts a high literacy rate, making it a key center for academic excellence and technical education in the state.
  • Hamirpur is well-connected by road and offers easy access to other major towns and cities in Himachal Pradesh.
  • It is also known for its adventurous trekking routes, temples, and vibrant local festivals that showcase the unique traditions of the Himachali people.

Connectivity

The NIT Hamirpur campus is situated at Anu in Hamirpur district of Himachal Pradesh and is nearly 4 Kms away from the main bus stand of Hamirpur on Hamirpur-Tauni Devi road. The city of Hamirpur is well connected with the rest of the country by road. Hamirpur is at a distance of about 450 Kms from Delhi and 200 Kms from Chandigarh.

By Road: Taxi/Bus services are available from Hamirpur to Una. The city is 450 Kms from Delhi and 200 Kms from Chandigarh. The nearest bus stand is about 4 Kms from the campus.

By Rail: Nearest broad gauge railway junction is at Una, Himachal Pradesh, about 80 Kms from Hamirpur. It's well linked to major cities, and the journey is around 9 hours from Old Delhi Railway Station. For train details, visit Indian Railways.

By Air: The nearest airport is Dharamshala (Gaggal, District Kangra) which is about 75 Kms (2 hours) from Hamirpur. Chandigarh International Airport is approximately 200 Kms away (4 hours).

Financial Incentives for International Students

The institute offers the following financial incentives for international students:

  • B.Tech Programme: One seat under G1, G2, and G3 in each programme
  • M.Sc Programme (All Branches) and MBA Programme: 01 seat under G1 category
  • Ph.D Programme: 02 seats in each department under G2 category

The details of G1, G2, and G3 categories are given below:
